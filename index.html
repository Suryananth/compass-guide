The 6-Year Arc
Three phases.
Each builds on the last.

Most families start planning in Grade 11. Compass starts in Grade 7 — giving your student a 4-year head start on every competitor.

Phase 1 — Preparation
Grades 7–8
  • Get on the right academic track before it matters
  • Enter first competitions — low stakes, high learning
  • Discover genuine interests before the pressure starts
  • Build habits that compound over 6 years
Phase 2 — Foundation
Grades 9–10
  • Connect with a research mentor or professional
  • Enter serious, nationally recognized competitions
  • Apply to selective summer programs
  • Build the record that college apps will tell
Phase 3 — Execution
Grades 11–12
  • Compete at national level — results that open doors
  • Secure paid internships and research positions
  • Submit college applications from a position of strength
  • Win scholarships — including ones others never find
Why Compass Exists
We figured it out the hard way.
You shouldn't have to.
"When my son started high school in Loudoun County, I realized I had no idea how the American college readiness system actually worked. Nobody tells you about ISEF in 7th grade. Nobody explains that the summer programs with the best outcomes close in February. Nobody maps out when to ask for recommendation letters, or what PSAT scores qualify for National Merit in Virginia. I figured it out — in real time, one missed deadline at a time. Compass is what I wish existed when we started."

Built by an immigrant parent with a product management background, Compass is designed specifically for families navigating a system that was never explained to them — South Asian, East African, Latin American, East Asian, Middle Eastern, Caribbean, Eastern European, and every ambitious family locked out by information, not by ability.

The students who end up at MIT, Stanford, and Johns Hopkins are not necessarily smarter. They started earlier, had better information, and had someone to show them the map. Compass is that map.
